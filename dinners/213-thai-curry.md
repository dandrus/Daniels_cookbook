## Thai Red Curry with Chicken & Vegetables

Servings: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 25 min | Total: ~40 min

### Ingredients
- 1½ lb chicken breast or thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1 cup chicken broth
- 1 cup full-fat coconut milk
- 3 Tbsp red curry paste
- 2 Tbsp fish sauce
- 1 zucchini, sliced into half-moons
- 2 bell peppers, sliced (any color)
- 1 white onion, sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 Tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 8 oz bamboo shoots, drained
- 2 Tbsp neutral oil (vegetable or coconut)

### Instructions
1. Heat oil in a large wok or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook 2–3 minutes until softened. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry 60 seconds until fragrant.
2. Add red curry paste and stir into the aromatics. Cook 1–2 minutes, pressing and folding into the oil, until the paste deepens in color and becomes very fragrant.
3. Add chicken in a single layer and sear 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lightly golden and mostly cooked through.
4. Pour in coconut milk and chicken broth; stir to combine. Stir in fish sauce. Bring to a gentle simmer.
5. Add bell peppers, zucchini, and bamboo shoots. Stir to coat everything in the sauce.
6. Simmer uncovered 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender but still have a slight bite and sauce has thickened slightly.
7. Taste and adjust seasoning — add more fish sauce for saltiness or more curry paste for heat. Serve hot over jasmine rice or with warm naan.

### Notes / Tips
- Start with 2 Tbsp curry paste for mild heat; go up to 4 Tbsp for a bold kick.
- For a creamier curry, use the full 14 oz can of coconut milk and reduce or omit the broth.
- A teaspoon of brown sugar or a splash of lime juice at the end can balance the flavors.
- Garnish with fresh Thai basil, cilantro, sliced red chili, or a squeeze of lime.
- Tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld together.
