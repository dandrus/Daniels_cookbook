## Thai Red Chicken Curry
*Pooja’s Cookery*

Servings: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 25 min | Total: ~40 min

### Ingredients
- 1½ lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, sliced
- 2 Tbsp red curry paste
- 1 Tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 can (13.5 oz) full-fat coconut milk
- 1 cup chicken broth (or water)
- 1 Tbsp fish sauce
- 1 Tbsp brown sugar
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup bamboo shoots, drained (optional)
- 2 kaffir lime leaves (optional)
- 1 Tbsp Thai basil or regular basil, chopped
- Salt, to taste

### Instructions
1. Heat oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat.
2. Add red curry paste and cook 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
3. Stir in half the coconut milk and cook until oil begins to separate.
4. Add chicken and stir to coat with curry sauce.
5. Pour in remaining coconut milk and chicken broth; bring to a gentle simmer.
6. Add fish sauce, brown sugar, bell pepper, bamboo shoots, and lime leaves (if using).
7. Simmer 12–15 minutes, until chicken is cooked through and sauce slightly thickened.
8. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt if needed.
9. Stir in basil just before serving.

### Notes / Tips
- Use **full-fat coconut milk** for best flavor and texture.
- Add sliced Thai chilies if you want more heat.
- Great served with jasmine rice or naan.
- Leftovers keep well for 2–3 days and reheat gently.
