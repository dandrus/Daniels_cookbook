## Sheet Pan Shrimp Boil
*Damn Delicious*

Servings: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 20 min | Total: ~30 min

### Ingredients
- 1 lb baby red potatoes, halved
- 3 Tbsp olive oil, divided
- Salt & black pepper, to taste
- 12 oz smoked sausage, sliced into rounds
- 2 ears corn, each cut into 4 pieces
- 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined (tails on or off)

**Seasoning & Finish**
- 1½ Tbsp Old Bay seasoning
- 2 Tbsp unsalted butter, melted
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 Tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- Lemon wedges, for serving

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Lightly grease a sheet pan or line with foil.
2. Toss potatoes with 1½ Tbsp olive oil, salt, and pepper; spread evenly on the pan.
3. Roast potatoes for 15 minutes.
4. In a bowl, toss sausage and corn with remaining olive oil.
5. Add sausage and corn to the pan and roast 10 minutes.
6. In another bowl, toss shrimp with Old Bay seasoning.
7. Add shrimp to the pan and roast an additional 8–10 minutes, until shrimp are pink and cooked through.
8. Stir together melted butter and garlic; drizzle over everything.
9. Sprinkle with parsley and serve hot with lemon wedges.

### Notes / Tips
- Use large or extra-large shrimp to avoid overcooking.
- Swap smoked sausage for andouille for extra Cajun heat.
- Line the pan with foil for easy cleanup.
- Leftovers reheat well in a skillet or air fryer.
