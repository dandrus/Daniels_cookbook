## Honey Soy Chicken Thighs
*Munchkin Time*

Servings: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 25 min | Total: ~35 min

### Ingredients
- 1½ lb boneless, skinless chicken thighs
- Salt & black pepper, to taste
- 1 Tbsp olive oil

**Honey Soy Sauce**
- ⅓ cup soy sauce (low sodium preferred)
- ¼ cup honey
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 Tbsp fresh ginger, minced (or 1 tsp ground ginger)
- 1 Tbsp rice vinegar or apple cider vinegar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)

**Garnish**
- Sliced green onions
- Sesame seeds

### Instructions
1. Season chicken thighs with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
3. Add chicken and cook 4–5 minutes per side until nicely browned and cooked through; remove and set aside.
4. In the same skillet, whisk together soy sauce, honey, garlic, ginger, vinegar, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes.
5. Bring sauce to a simmer and cook 2–3 minutes until slightly thickened.
6. Return chicken to the skillet and toss to coat in the sauce.
7. Simmer 2–3 minutes until sauce clings to the chicken.
8. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds before serving.

### Notes / Tips
- Boneless thighs cook faster and stay juicy — great choice.
- Don’t over-reduce the sauce; it thickens quickly from the honey.
- Excellent over jasmine rice or rice noodles.
- Add steamed broccoli or snap peas directly to the pan at the end if desired.
