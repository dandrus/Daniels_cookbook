## Copycat Zuppa Toscana
*Will Cook For Smiles*

Servings: 6 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: 30 min | Total: ~40 min

### Ingredients
- 1 lb Italian sausage (mild or hot)
- 4 slices bacon, chopped
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 4 cups russet potatoes, sliced or diced
- 1 tsp Italian seasoning
- ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
- Salt & black pepper, to taste
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 2 cups kale, chopped

### Instructions
1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, cook bacon over medium heat until crisp; remove and set aside, leaving grease in the pot.
2. Add Italian sausage and cook until browned, breaking it up as it cooks.
3. Add onion and sauté until softened, about 4–5 minutes.
4. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Pour in chicken broth and add potatoes, Italian seasoning, and red pepper flakes.
6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer 15–20 minutes, or until potatoes are tender.
7. Stir in heavy cream and kale; simmer 3–5 minutes until kale is wilted.
8. Taste and season with salt and pepper as needed.
9. Serve hot topped with reserved bacon.

### Notes / Tips
- Slice potatoes thin for faster cooking and better texture.
- Substitute spinach for kale if preferred.
- Use hot Italian sausage for a spicier version.
- This soup thickens as it sits; add broth when reheating.
