## Andrus Gumbo
Family recipe

Servings: 6–8 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 1½–2+ hr | Total: ~2 hr+

### Ingredients

Roux
- All-purpose flour
- Neutral oil (vegetable or canola)

Aromatics
- Onion, diced (amount to taste)

Liquid
- Water (enough to fill about ¾ of the pot)

Protein & Add-ins (use what you want)
- Chicken
- Andouille or smoked sausage
- Shrimp or other seafood (if making seafood gumbo)

Seasoning
- Salt
- Black pepper
- Cajun seasoning, to taste

### Instructions
1. In a heavy-bottomed, non-nonstick pot, mix flour and oil until the consistency of Elmer’s glue.
2. Make enough roux to cover about ¼ of the bottom of the pot.
3. Cook roux over medium-high heat, stirring continuously.
4. Continue cooking until desired color:
   - Caramel brown for chicken and sausage gumbo
   - Very dark, almost black for seafood gumbo
5. Add diced onions directly to the hot roux.
6. Cook onions until soft and fragrant.
7. Add water to fill the pot about ¾ full.
8. Bring to a boil, stirring well to incorporate the roux.
9. Add remaining ingredients, except shrimp if using.
10. Reduce heat and simmer at least 1 hour (longer is better).
11. If making seafood gumbo, add shrimp during the last 15 minutes only.
12. Taste and adjust seasoning before serving.

### Notes / Tips
- Stir constantly during the roux; it will burn if neglected.
- Avoid nonstick pots; cast iron or heavy stainless steel works best.
- Darker roux equals deeper flavor but requires full attention.
- Gumbo is always better the next day.
- Serve over rice if desired.
