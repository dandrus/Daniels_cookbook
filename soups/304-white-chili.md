## White Chicken Chili
*Cooking Classy*

Servings: 6 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 40 min | Total: ~55 min

### Ingredients
- 1 Tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts or thighs, diced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp chili powder
- ½ tsp paprika
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- ¼ tsp cayenne pepper (optional)
- Salt & black pepper, to taste
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 2 cans (15 oz each) cannellini or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 can (4 oz) diced green chiles
- 1 cup frozen corn
- ½ cup sour cream
- ½ cup heavy cream

**Optional Toppings**
- Shredded Monterey Jack or Pepper Jack cheese
- Chopped cilantro
- Tortilla strips or crushed chips
- Lime wedges

### Instructions
1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
2. Add onion and sauté until softened, about 4–5 minutes.
3. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. Add diced chicken and cook until lightly browned.
5. Stir in cumin, chili powder, paprika, oregano, cayenne, salt, and pepper.
6. Pour in chicken broth and bring to a simmer.
7. Add beans, green chiles, and corn; simmer 20–25 minutes until chicken is cooked through.
8. Reduce heat to low and stir in sour cream and heavy cream until smooth.
9. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
10. Serve hot with desired toppings.

### Notes / Tips
- Chicken thighs add extra richness, but breasts work well too.
- For thicker chili, mash a portion of the beans before adding dairy.
- Don’t boil after adding sour cream to prevent curdling.
- Excellent for leftovers and freezes well without toppings.
