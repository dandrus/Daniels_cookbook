## Lumpia (Filipino Spring Rolls)
*Kawaling Pinoy*

Servings: 8–10 (about 30 rolls) | Prep: 30 min | Cook: 20 min | Total: ~50 min

### Ingredients

**Filling**
- 1 lb ground pork (or ground beef)
- 1 cup carrots, finely shredded
- 1 cup cabbage, finely shredded
- ½ cup green onions, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp oyster sauce
- ½ tsp black pepper
- Salt, to taste

**For Rolling & Frying**
- 30 lumpia wrappers (found in Asian grocery stores, kept frozen)
- 1 egg, beaten (for sealing)
- Vegetable oil, for frying

**Dipping Sauce**
- ¼ cup soy sauce
- 2 Tbsp white vinegar
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- Pinch of sugar
- Sliced chili (optional)

### Instructions
1. In a large bowl, combine ground pork, carrots, cabbage, green onions, garlic, soy sauce, oyster sauce, pepper, and salt. Mix until well combined.
2. Lay one lumpia wrapper flat on a clean surface.
3. Place about 2 Tbsp of filling near the bottom edge.
4. Fold the bottom edge over the filling, then fold in the sides, and roll tightly upward.
5. Seal the edge with a dab of beaten egg.
6. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling.
7. Heat 2–3 inches of oil in a deep skillet or pot to 350°F.
8. Fry lumpia in batches for 3–4 minutes per side, until golden brown and crispy.
9. Drain on paper towels.
10. Mix dipping sauce ingredients together in a small bowl.
11. Serve hot with dipping sauce.

### Notes / Tips
- Keep unused wrappers covered with a damp towel to prevent drying out.
- Lumpia can be assembled and frozen uncooked; fry directly from frozen, adding 1–2 minutes to cook time.
- Pairs especially well with Chamoru Red Rice and Filipino BBQ Chicken.
- For a lighter version, bake at 400°F for 18–20 minutes, flipping halfway.
