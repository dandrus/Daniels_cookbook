## French Toast Casserole
*Spend With Pennies*

Servings: 8 | Prep: 15 min | Chill: 8 hr | Cook: 45 min | Total: ~9 hr

### Ingredients

**Casserole**
- 1 loaf brioche or French bread, cut into 1-inch cubes (about 10 cups)
- 8 large eggs
- 2 cups whole milk
- ½ cup heavy cream
- ⅓ cup granulated sugar
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 1½ tsp cinnamon
- ¼ tsp nutmeg
- Pinch of salt

**Streusel Topping**
- ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
- ⅓ cup brown sugar, packed
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- ¼ cup cold butter, cubed

**For Serving**
- Maple syrup
- Powdered sugar

### Instructions
1. Grease a 9×13 baking dish. Spread bread cubes evenly in the dish.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, cream, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
3. Pour custard mixture evenly over the bread, pressing bread down to absorb.
4. Cover and refrigerate overnight (or at least 8 hours).
5. When ready to bake, preheat oven to 350°F.
6. In a small bowl, mix flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon for the streusel.
7. Cut in cold butter until crumbly.
8. Sprinkle streusel evenly over the casserole.
9. Bake uncovered 40–45 minutes, until puffed, golden, and set in the center.
10. Let rest 5 minutes before serving.
11. Dust with powdered sugar and serve with maple syrup.

### Notes / Tips
- Brioche gives the richest result; day-old bread absorbs the custard better.
- Can assemble up to 24 hours ahead.
- Cover loosely with foil for the first 20 minutes if the top browns too quickly.
- Add fresh berries or sliced bananas on top before baking for variation.
