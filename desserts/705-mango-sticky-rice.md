## Mango Sticky Rice
*Hot Thai Kitchen*

Servings: 4 | Prep: 10 min | Soak: 4 hr | Cook: 30 min | Total: ~4 hr 40 min

### Ingredients

**Sticky Rice**
- 2 cups glutinous (sticky) rice
- 1¾ cups full-fat coconut milk
- ¼ cup granulated sugar
- ½ tsp salt

**Coconut Sauce**
- ½ cup full-fat coconut milk
- 2 Tbsp granulated sugar
- ¼ tsp salt
- 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 1 Tbsp cold water

**For Serving**
- 2 ripe mangoes, peeled and sliced
- Sesame seeds or mung beans (optional garnish)

### Instructions
1. Rinse sticky rice until water runs clear. Soak in cold water for at least 4 hours or overnight.
2. Drain rice and steam in a bamboo steamer or steamer basket lined with cheesecloth for 20–25 minutes, until translucent and tender.
3. While rice steams, combine coconut milk, sugar, and salt in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir until sugar dissolves; do not boil.
4. Transfer steamed rice to a large bowl. Pour warm coconut milk mixture over rice and stir gently.
5. Cover and let rice absorb the coconut milk for 15–20 minutes.
6. For the sauce, combine coconut milk, sugar, and salt in a saucepan over medium heat. Stir in cornstarch slurry and cook until slightly thickened. Remove from heat.
7. Serve rice warm alongside sliced mango, drizzled with coconut sauce.
8. Garnish with sesame seeds or mung beans if desired.

### Notes / Tips
- Glutinous rice (also called sweet rice) is essential — regular rice will not work.
- Find glutinous rice and bamboo steamers at Asian grocery stores.
- Mangoes should be very ripe and sweet for best results.
- Rice can be made ahead and reheated gently with a splash of coconut milk.
