## Bread Pudding with Vanilla Sauce
*Taste of Home*

Servings: 9 | Prep: 20 min | Soak: 15 min | Cook: 45 min | Total: ~1 hr 20 min

### Ingredients

**Bread Pudding**
- 8 cups day-old French bread, cubed (about 1 loaf)
- 3 large eggs
- 2 cups whole milk
- 1 cup heavy cream
- ¾ cup granulated sugar
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 1½ tsp cinnamon
- ¼ tsp nutmeg
- Pinch of salt
- ½ cup raisins (optional)
- 3 Tbsp unsalted butter, melted

**Vanilla Sauce**
- ½ cup unsalted butter
- 1 cup powdered sugar
- 1 large egg, beaten
- 2 tsp vanilla extract
- 2 Tbsp bourbon or whiskey (optional)

### Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Grease a 9×13 baking dish and spread bread cubes evenly inside.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together eggs, milk, cream, sugar, vanilla, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt.
3. Pour custard over bread and press bread down gently. Let soak 15 minutes.
4. Scatter raisins over the top if using, and drizzle with melted butter.
5. Bake uncovered 40–45 minutes, until set in the center and golden on top.
6. While pudding bakes, make the vanilla sauce: melt butter in a small saucepan over medium-low heat.
7. Whisk in powdered sugar until smooth.
8. Stir a spoonful of the hot butter mixture into the beaten egg to temper it, then whisk egg into the saucepan.
9. Cook 2–3 minutes, stirring constantly, until slightly thickened.
10. Remove from heat and stir in vanilla and bourbon if using.
11. Serve pudding warm, drizzled generously with vanilla sauce.

### Notes / Tips
- Day-old or stale bread absorbs the custard without falling apart.
- Brioche or challah makes a richer, more indulgent version.
- The bourbon in the sauce is optional but adds great depth.
- Leftovers reheat well in the oven at 325°F covered with foil.
