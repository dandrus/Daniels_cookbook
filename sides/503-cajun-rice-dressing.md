## Cajun Rice Dressing
*Louisiana Woman Blog*

Servings: 6–8 | Prep: 15 min | Cook: 45 min | Total: ~1 hr

### Ingredients

**Meat Mixture**
- 1 lb ground beef
- ½ lb ground pork
- 1 chicken gizzard, finely chopped (optional but traditional)

**Vegetables & Seasoning**
- 1 Tbsp vegetable oil (if needed)
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 1 bell pepper, finely diced
- 2 celery ribs, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp Cajun seasoning
- ½ tsp black pepper
- Salt, to taste

**Rice**
- 3 cups cooked long-grain white rice
- 2–3 cups beef or chicken broth (as needed)

### Instructions
1. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, cook ground beef, pork, and chopped gizzard over medium heat until browned.
2. Drain excess grease if necessary.
3. Add onion, bell pepper, and celery; cook until vegetables are softened, about 5–7 minutes.
4. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Season with Cajun seasoning, black pepper, and salt.
6. Stir in cooked rice until evenly combined.
7. Gradually add broth, stirring, until rice is moist but not soupy.
8. Reduce heat to low and simmer 10–15 minutes to let flavors meld.
9. Taste and adjust seasoning before serving.

### Notes / Tips
- This is classic **dirty rice–style dressing**; finely chopped meat and veggies are key.
- Chicken livers can be used instead of gizzards for a richer, more traditional version.
- Great side for fried chicken, pork chops, or holiday meals.
- Leftovers reheat well with a splash of broth.
