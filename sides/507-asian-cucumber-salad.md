## Asian Cucumber Salad
*Pickled Plum*

Servings: 4 | Prep: 15 min | Rest: 10 min | Total: ~25 min

### Ingredients

**Salad**
- 2 large English cucumbers (or 4 Persian cucumbers)
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 green onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp sesame seeds, toasted

**Dressing**
- 2 Tbsp soy sauce
- 1 Tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 Tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp chili garlic sauce or sriracha (adjust to taste)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, grated

### Instructions
1. Slice cucumbers into thin rounds or half-moons.
2. Place in a colander, toss with salt, and let sit 10 minutes to draw out excess moisture.
3. Pat cucumbers dry with paper towels.
4. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, sugar, chili garlic sauce, garlic, and ginger.
5. Toss cucumbers with dressing until well coated.
6. Top with sliced green onions and sesame seeds.
7. Serve immediately or refrigerate up to 1 hour before serving.

### Notes / Tips
- Salting the cucumbers is key — it keeps the salad from getting watery.
- For a fun texture, smash cucumbers with the flat of a knife instead of slicing.
- Pairs well with Filipino BBQ Chicken, Thai Red Curry, or Honey Soy Chicken.
- Best eaten the same day; cucumbers release more liquid as they sit.
