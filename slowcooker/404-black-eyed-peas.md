## Southern Black-Eyed Peas
*Butter Be Ready*

Servings: 6–8 | Prep: 10 min | Cook: ~1 hr | Total: ~1 hr 10 min

### Ingredients
- 1 lb dried black-eyed peas, rinsed and picked over
- 6 cups chicken broth (or water)
- 1 smoked ham hock (or smoked turkey leg/wing)
- 1 Tbsp olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp Cajun seasoning
- ½ tsp black pepper
- ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
- Salt, to taste
- Hot sauce, for serving (optional)

### Instructions
1. Rinse black-eyed peas thoroughly and set aside.
2. Heat olive oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
3. Add diced onion and sauté until softened, about 4–5 minutes.
4. Add garlic and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Stir in smoked paprika, Cajun seasoning, black pepper, and red pepper flakes.
6. Add black-eyed peas, chicken broth, and ham hock. Stir to combine.
7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered for 50–60 minutes, stirring occasionally.
8. Remove ham hock, shred meat, discard bone and excess fat, and return meat to the pot.
9. Season with salt to taste and simmer an additional 5–10 minutes.
10. Serve hot with cornbread or over rice.

### Notes / Tips
- No soaking required for black-eyed peas.
- Smoked turkey is a great lighter alternative to ham hock.
- For thicker peas, lightly mash a small portion before serving.
- Even better the next day once flavors settle.
